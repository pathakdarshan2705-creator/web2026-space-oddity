Notes on a Small Web

Ada Lovelace March 1843

The web began as a pile of documents linked to one another, made by people who mostly wanted to be read. You could see the whole of a page in its source, and copy what you liked.

Somewhere along the way the pages got heavier and the people got quieter. But the old web is still there for anyone who wants to make a corner of it by hand.

A page you can read from top to bottom is worth more than one you have to fight.

None of this needs a framework. A folder, a text file, and a little patience will do. That is the whole trick, and it has not changed in thirty years.
